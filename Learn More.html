Emergency Response

The emergency response with a natural disaster is usually divided into cycle. This cycle is consisted of the 4 phases which are:

Mitigation

Mitigation is the phase where steps to improve safety and moves are made to find multiple ways to reduce vulnerable cities so another disaster will not have as many potential casualities. These can include strengthening buildings, creating stronger infrastructures that can withstand the disasater, improved use of the surrounding land as zones of safety and more.

Preparedness

Preparedness is the phase where precautions are taken and communities are educated on what to do when a disaster occurs, the proper ways to respond and the ideas of how to recover. These can include advertisements on safety, new workplace policies for safety, meetings for communities to educate themselves and several other ideas.

Response

Response is the phase of response immediately when a disaster happens and rescuing surviviors and supplying those in need. These include charities such as the Red Cross supplying those affected, clearing up debris, emergecny repairs, humanitarian donations such as food, shelter and clothing for those who are in need and other methods.

Recovery

Recovery is the phase where the a community is restoring themselves and trying to get their humanitarian, economic and environmental needs satisfied. This involves charity rasied money for reparations and survivors, local resources of food shelter and clothing, more government trades and partnerships to improve economy for the community or country affected and more actions.
